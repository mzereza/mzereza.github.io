Hello, I'm Miguel Peñaranda.

Cybersecurity Engineering student at URJC. I've been passionate about cybersecurity for as long as I can remember, and I love understanding how things work at a low level and finding their weak points.

I really enjoy learning every day, especially through CTFs. They are an excellent way to learn because you do not only discover new concepts, you also internalize them by applying them. This blog is my public notebook: I write about what I learn, the challenges I solve, and the concepts I find interesting.

Offensive security has many disciplines. I am especially interested in those focused on low-level concepts, particularly binary exploitation (pwn). Still, I do not limit myself to that and I also enjoy learning about areas like web hacking and red teaming.
